# St John's College x Cosmos Institute Summer Symposium

**Format:** Summer symposium
**Duration:** 3 days (June 30 - July 2, 2025)
**Location:** St John's College
**Partners:** St John's College, Cosmos Institute

---

## Overview

A concentrated reading seminar exploring the intellectual foundations of computation and technology through primary sources, from medieval algebra to modern philosophy of technology. The symposium follows the St John's "Great Books" discussion format.

## Themes

1. **Origins of Algorithm**: From al-Khwarizmi's algebra (the word "algorithm" derives from his name) to Knuth's modern formalization
2. **The Question of Technology**: Heidegger's concept of *Gestell* (Enframing) and technology as a mode of revealing
3. **Computation as Universal Language**: Leibniz's dream of a *characteristica universalis* and binary arithmetic as foundations for modern computing
4. **Technological Ethics**: Hans Jonas on human responsibility in the technological age
5. **The Promethean Myth**: Shelley's *Frankenstein* as meditation on creation, responsibility, and the limits of human knowledge
