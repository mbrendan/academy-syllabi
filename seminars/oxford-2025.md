# Oxford 2025: AI x Philosophy Seminar

**Format:** Academic seminar
**Duration:** 8 weeks (Trinity Term 2025)
**Location:** University of Oxford
**Instructors:** Philipp Koralus (HAI Lab) and Brendan McCord (Cosmos Institute)

---

## Overview

Co-delivered by Professor Philipp Koralus, Director of the Laboratory for Human-Centered AI (HAI Lab) at the University of Oxford, and Brendan McCord, Chair of the Cosmos Institute, this seminar brings together leading thinkers from philosophy and the cutting edge of AI development.

Each week, distinguished visitors from organizations including Midjourney, Google DeepMind, Prime Intellect, Apple, and academia engage with seminar participants in substantive dialogue about how we might embed human flourishing into the global technology development pipeline from first principles.

From truth-seeking AI systems to collective intelligence, from privacy concerns to democratic deliberation, we explore how philosophical frameworks can guide the development of technologies that enhance rather than diminish human autonomy.

**Independent project opportunity:** Grants awarded to select students for independent summer building projects on related themes via Cosmos Institute's fast grants program.

## Syllabus

### Week 1: Truth-seeking AI

**Instructors:** Philipp Koralus (HAI Lab) and Brendan McCord (Cosmos)

How should AI systems be designed to support truth-seeking?

**Readings:**
- Plato, *Theaetetus*, excerpts (149A-152A; 189A-190A)
- Mill, *On Liberty*, Ch. 2, "Of Liberty of Thought and Discussion" (excerpts)
- Koralus, "The Philosophic Turn for AI Agents: Replacing Centralized Digital Rhetoric with Decentralized Truth-Seeking"

## Visitor Biographies

**Jules Desai** is a philosopher, computer scientist, and electronic musician. His research spans advanced reasoning capabilities in LLMs, neurally-inspired machine learning models, and the intersection of logic, AI, and the philosophies of Kant, Heidegger, and Wittgenstein. He holds a BPhil and MPhysPhil from the University of Oxford.

**Helen Nissenbaum** is the Andrew H. and Ann R. Tisch Professor at Cornell Tech and Director of the Digital Life Initiative. Her research focuses on ethics, policy, law, and technology, particularly privacy and values in technology design. Author of *Privacy in Context* and *Obfuscation: A User's Guide for Privacy and Protest*.

**Carina Peng** is a machine learning engineer at Apple. She studied Computer Science and Philosophy at Harvard College and has worked at Tesla, WHO, and QuantCo.

**Vincent Weisser** is Founder & CEO of Prime Intellect, a decentralized AI platform. He also serves as Advisor at Molecule AG and is a Founding Steward at VitaDAO.

**Ivan Vendrov** leads the collective intelligence team at Midjourney. Previously at Anthropic and Google Research. Holds degrees from University of Saskatchewan and University of Toronto.

**Christopher Summerfield** is Professor of Cognitive Neuroscience at Oxford and Staff Research Scientist at Google DeepMind. Author of *Natural General Intelligence* and *These Strange New Minds: How AI Learned to Talk and What It Means*.

**Michael Henry (MH) Tessler** is a Senior Research Scientist at Google DeepMind working on LLM safety and alignment. Previously postdoctoral researcher at MIT and PhD from Stanford Psychology.

**Bethanie Drake-Maples** is a PhD student at Stanford HAI researching embodied AI and human-machine interface systems for cognitive development and education. Previously at Google AI and Google X.

## Instructor Biographies

**Philipp Koralus** is the McCord Professor of Philosophy and AI at the University of Oxford and Director of the Oxford Human-Centered AI Lab (HAI Lab). His research focuses on the nature of reason, including his recent book *Reason and Inquiry*. He has developed a new mathematical framework for understanding human-like reason that sheds light on standards of rationality for AI systems. Ph.D. in Philosophy and Neuroscience from Princeton University.

**Brendan McCord** is the founder and Chair of Cosmos Institute and Cosmos Holdings. In the private sector, he was the founding CEO of two AI startups acquired for $400 million. In the public sector, he was the principal founder of the first applied AI organization for the US Department of Defense and author of its first AI strategy. Graduate of MIT and Harvard Business School, former Visiting Fellow at St Catherine's College, Oxford.
