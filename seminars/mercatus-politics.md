# Mercatus Mill Fellowship: On Politics

**Format:** Fellowship reading seminar
**Duration:** 6 sessions
**Location:** Austin, TX (Four Seasons Hotel)
**Date:** November 30 – December 2, 2023
**Partner:** Mercatus Center at George Mason University

---

## Overview

Part of the John Stuart Mill Fellowship program at the Mercatus Center, this seminar explores the foundations of political liberty, constitutional order, law, and the limits of state power through Mill, Hayek, and Buchanan.

---

## Syllabus

### Session 1: Liberty of Thought and Discussion

**Readings:**

**Mill, John Stuart. *J.S. Mill On Liberty and Other Writings***
- Chapter 1, "Introductory" (pages 5–18)
- Chapter 2, "Of the Liberty of Thought and Discussion" (pages 19–55)

---

### Session 2: Individuality and the Limits of Authority

**Readings:**

**Mill, John Stuart. *J.S. Mill On Liberty and Other Writings***
- Chapter 3, "Of Individuality, as One of the Elements of Wellbeing" (pages 56–74)
- Chapter 4, "Of the Limits to the Authority of Society over the Individual" (pages 75–93)
- Chapter 5, "Applications" (pages 94–115)

---

### Session 3: Cosmos, Taxis, and Constitutional Order

**Readings:**

**Hayek, F.A. *The Collected Works of F.A. Hayek*, Vol. 19: *Law, Legislation, and Liberty***

- Chapter 1, "The Constitutional Imperative" (pages 3–22)
- Chapter 2, "The Contractarian Vision" (pages 23–37)
- Chapter 3, "The Myth of Benevolence" (pages 38–52)
- Chapter 9, "Is Constitutional Revolution Possible in Democracy?" (pages 149–167)

### Session 4: Principles, Expediency, and the Law of Liberty

**Readings:**

- Chapter 3, "Principles and Expediency" (pages 79–96)

### Session 5: Justice and Distribution

**Readings:**

### Session 6: The Limits of Liberty and the Threat of Leviathan

## About the Mill Fellowship

The John Stuart Mill Fellowship at the Mercatus Center brings together emerging scholars and practitioners to engage with classical liberal ideas and their application to contemporary challenges.
