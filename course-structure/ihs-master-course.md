## Module 1: Intellectual Origins of Technology

Throughout history, major scientific discoveries have transformed how we see ourselves as human beings. Each breakthrough forced us to redraw the map of our place in the cosmos:

- Copernicus dislodged us from our central position in the universe
- Bacon revealed we could master nature for our purposes
- Newton proved the world follows mathematical laws, not human intuition
- Darwin situated us within the animal kingdom

Now we're living through another such moment—perhaps the culminating point of the modern technological project: **the Age of Turing**. As artificial intelligence reshapes our world, we are invited to return to the history of ideas to reconsider the place of technology in human life: what good it produces, what danger it poses, and what should be its relation to human guidance or morality.

## Module 2: Human Autonomy

In every technological revolution, we face a choice: build for freedom or watch as others build for control. While AI promises to liberate us for higher pursuits by "extending the number of important operations which we can perform without thinking," it also threatens to become an "autocomplete for life," offering packaged responses that slowly erode our autonomy.

The ability to meaningfully self-determine is a capacity: one that requires more than just "agency"—we need both **agency** (to effectively achieve our goals) and **autonomy** (to deliberate on and choose for ourselves which goals to pursue).

## Module 3: Truth-Seeking

AI already drafts our sentences, sorts our inbox, and picks the next song. Soon the same technology could determine what ideas ever reach our mind—or form within it. Two futures lie ahead:

**Algorithmic tyranny:** AI becomes a shadow censor. Hidden ranking rules throttle dissent, and liability fears chill speech. Default recommendations and flattering prompts dull our judgment, and people stop asking "why."

**Partner in truth-seeking:** AI surfaces counter-arguments, flags open questions, and prompts us to check the evidence. Errors are chipped away. Knowledge grows. Our freedom—and habit—to question stays intact, and even thrives.

We examine what principles should be coded into systems that have the potential to become the hidden structure that shapes human thought.

## Module 4: Decentralization

## Module 5: Philosopher-Builder

Too often technologists build products based on assumptions they haven't deeply examined. This is especially true in the field of artificial intelligence. Philosophy reminds us that even our most confident answers—about what's worth building—are usually more fragile than they seem.

In the tech world, this kind of unexamined thinking has led to an era focused more on engagement and convenience over meaningful human flourishing. Philosophical inquiry forces us to ask better, deeper questions and rely on moral reflection into what and how we build.

**Enter the Philosopher-Builder.**

## The Vision

> Great leaps in science and technology came from people who refuse to accept inertia... The types of people we need—philosopher-builders—and their inquiry and entrepreneurial endeavors won't happen in a vacuum.

Inquiry has always been supported by institutional structures that facilitate collaborative investigation: from Aristotle's Lyceum, to Franklin's Junto, to the Republic of Letters. The Academy follows in this tradition—creating spaces where deep questioning and practical building occur in concert.
